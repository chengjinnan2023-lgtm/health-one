## Today's Goal

> We're testing our new Health One system with real customers. The goal is simple: **complete the full service loop for at least one customer, and see if the system helps or gets in the way.**

This is NOT a test of you. This is a test of the system. If something is confusing or slow, that's the system's problem — not yours. Tell us.

---

## How to Operate (8 Steps)

```
1. Login → use your staff account
2. Search customer (or click "+ New Customer" if new)
3. Activate (click "Activate 健康元" — just once)
4. Record Concern (pick a category + type what customer said)
5. New Service (pick service type + type what you did)
6. Feedback (3 quick questions — feeling, satisfaction, return)
7. Follow-Up (pick method + date)
8. Back to Summary (check Timeline has today's events)
```

**That's it.** The Quick Reference Card on your desk shows the same steps.

**Required fields** are marked with * on screen. Everything else is optional.

---

## If Something Goes Wrong

**Don't panic. Serve the customer first.**

1. If a button doesn't work → wait 5 seconds, try again
2. If the page looks wrong → refresh (F5 or ⌘+R)
3. If you can't fix it in 30 seconds → **stop using the system**

**Use paper instead:**

- Write down: customer name, service, feedback
- We'll enter it later
- The customer's experience is more important than the data

**Tell us after:**

- What step was confusing?
- What took too long?
- What would make this easier?

---

## What NOT to Do

❌ Don't enter medical diagnoses (e.g., "cervical spondylosis")
❌ Don't enter customer ID numbers or payment info
❌ Don't share your password
❌ Don't try to fix the computer yourself
❌ Don't skip asking for customer consent
❌ Don't worry about making mistakes — Timeline entries can't be changed, and that's OK

---

## After Today

We'll ask you 4 questions:

1. Which step was easiest?
2. Which step was hardest?
3. Did the system slow you down?
4. Would you use this again?

Your honest feedback is the most important result of today.

## Thank You

> You're helping us build a system that actually works for real stores. Whatever happens today — smooth or bumpy — we learn and improve. Thank you for being part of this.
