## 1. Purpose

This Charter defines the foundational commitment, scope, authority, and operating discipline of the Health One project.

It exists to ensure that Health One develops as a coherent long-term health ecosystem, not as a collection of disconnected ideas, tools, documents, or experiments.

The Charter provides a stable reference for:

1. Project purpose.
2. Founder authority.
3. Governance boundaries.
4. Product direction.
5. AI collaboration.
6. Knowledge base development.
7. Long-term decision discipline.

---

## 2. Project Commitment

Health One is committed to building a continuously evolving health management ecosystem.

The project is not committed merely to building software.

It is committed to building a system that connects:

1. Personal health identity.
2. Long-term health memory.
3. AI health companions.
4. Real stores.
5. Real services.
6. Health managers.
7. Devices.
8. Knowledge.
9. Partners.
10. Ecosystem learning.

The project should always remain connected to real health service scenarios and real customer value.

---

## 3. Founder Authority

The Founder holds final approval authority over the Health One project.

The Founder has authority over:

1. Project direction.
2. Business positioning.
3. Product priorities.
4. Major architecture direction.
5. Baseline document approval.
6. External cooperation strategy.
7. Resource allocation.
8. Timing of execution.

No AI collaborator, tool, document, or workflow may override Founder authority.

---

## 4. Chief Architect Responsibility

The Chief Architect is responsible for protecting the structural coherence of the project.

The Chief Architect should:

1. Maintain the project architecture.
2. Review strategic consistency.
3. Protect document hierarchy.
4. Identify premature complexity.
5. Prevent context drift.
6. Distinguish between discussion, review, and release.
7. Decide whether a topic is mature enough for formalization.
8. Recommend when to proceed and when to pause.

The Chief Architect provides structure and judgment, but does not replace the Founder.

---

## 5. Release Engineer Responsibility

The Release Engineer is responsible for formal release execution.

The Release Engineer should:

1. Check Git status before every operation.
2. Create or update only approved files.
3. Preserve approved content.
4. Commit changes clearly.
5. Push changes to the remote repository.
6. Report the final result.
7. Stop when repository state is unclear.

The Release Engineer must not act as an autonomous business strategist, product architect, or decision maker unless explicitly assigned that role.

At the current stage, Claude Code acts primarily as Release Engineer.

## 6. Project Scope

Health One includes the following scope:

1. Personal health identity.
2. Health memory.
3. AI health companion.
4. Store workbench.
5. Store service management.
6. Device and service connection.
7. Health service follow-up.
8. Knowledge base.
9. AI collaboration system.
10. Business and partner models.
11. Product and architecture design.
12. Engineering implementation.
13. Long-term ecosystem development.

This scope may evolve through formal review and release.

---

## 7. Current MVP Scope

The current MVP should focus on the shortest complete health value loop.

The MVP should include:

1. 健康元.
2. AI health companion.
3. Store workbench.
4. Service or device connection.
5. Service record.
6. Follow-up loop.
7. Basic knowledge base.

The MVP should prove that Health One can create a repeatable health service loop before expanding into more complex systems.

---

## 8. Current MVP Exclusions

The current MVP should not prioritize:

1. Full marketplace.
2. Complex franchise system.
3. Token mechanism.
4. Blockchain.
5. Full contribution monetization.
6. Full digital human system.
7. Overbuilt CRM.
8. Overbuilt ERP.
9. Premature large-scale automation.

These topics may be reserved for future design, but they should not distract the early value loop.

---

## 9. Knowledge Base Commitment

Health One is developed as a knowledge-driven project.

The project knowledge base is a core asset.

It should include:

1. Governance documents.
2. Foundation documents.
3. Product documents.
4. Architecture documents.
5. AI documents.
6. Business documents.
7. Engineering documents.
8. Research documents.
9. Logs.
10. Release history.

Git is the formal carrier of this knowledge base.

All formal knowledge must be reviewed, approved, committed, and pushed to Git.

---

## 10. Source of Truth

The formal source of truth is:

> The latest approved Git repository content.

Priority order:

1. Latest approved Git content.
2. Latest committed Baseline document.
3. Release log.
4. Founder's explicit latest instruction.
5. Chief Architect's reviewed recommendation.
6. Informal chat history.
7. AI memory.

If informal context conflicts with Git, Git has priority.

---

## 11. Decision Discipline

Health One decisions should follow this discipline:

1. Discuss before deciding.
2. Review before releasing.
3. Release before treating as official.
4. Record material changes.
5. Preserve traceability.
6. Avoid mixing unrelated decisions.
7. Avoid premature complexity.
8. Protect long-term coherence.

The project should not move faster than its foundation can support.

---

## 12. AI Collaboration Commitment

Health One may use multiple AI collaborators and multiple AI workspaces.

However, AI collaboration must remain governed.

AI collaborators must:

1. Understand their assigned role.
2. Read relevant project documents.
3. Follow GOV-001, GOV-002, and GOV-003.
4. Avoid inventing official project facts.
5. Avoid modifying Baseline documents without approval.
6. Return formal decisions to the central Git-based process.
7. Report uncertainty clearly.
8. Respect Founder authority.

AI should accelerate Health One without fragmenting it.

---

## 13. Multi-Workspace Model

Health One may use specialized AI workspaces, such as:

1. Main Architecture Window.
2. Release Engineering Workspace.
3. Story Studio.
4. Brand Studio.
5. Business Studio.
6. Product Studio.
7. Engineering Studio.
8. Research Studio.

Each workspace may explore its own topic.

But no workspace may independently create official project truth.

Formal decisions must return to the central governance and Git release process.

---

## 14. Product Development Discipline

Health One product development should follow the value loop.

A product feature should be prioritized when it strengthens:

1. Customer health understanding.
2. Customer health memory.
3. Store service capability.
4. Service follow-up.
5. Trust.
6. Reuse and retention.
7. Real business operation.
8. Long-term learning.

A feature should be delayed when it mainly adds complexity without strengthening the core value loop.

---

## 15. Business Development Discipline

Health One business development should remain connected to real service and real stores.

Business exploration may include:

1. Store cooperation.
2. Coach partner cooperation.
3. Health service packages.
4. Device usage models.
5. Partner ecosystems.
6. Financing and investment resources.
7. Content and customer acquisition.
8. Franchise or alliance models.

However, business expansion should not outrun the ability to deliver consistent service value.

---

## 16. Engineering Discipline

Engineering should support validated product and business direction.

Engineering should not be used to prematurely overbuild unverified ideas.

Engineering principles include:

1. Start with the value loop.
2. Keep architecture understandable.
3. Prefer incremental delivery.
4. Preserve data ownership principles.
5. Protect customer trust.
6. Support AI and knowledge base integration.
7. Maintain traceability.
8. Avoid irreversible complexity too early.

---

## 17. Trust Commitment

Trust is a core asset of Health One.

The project must protect trust across:

1. Customer data.
2. Health memory.
3. AI responses.
4. Store service.
5. Staff behavior.
6. Follow-up records.
7. Partner cooperation.
8. Business communication.

Health One should not sacrifice trust for short-term conversion.

---

## 18. Customer Ownership Principle

The customer owns the 健康元.

This means:

1. The customer should be treated as the owner of their health identity.
2. Health memory should not be treated as merely platform-owned traffic data.
3. Store access should be based on service authorization.
4. Platform analysis should respect permission and responsibility.
5. AI should operate within authorized context.

This principle should guide future product, architecture, and business design.

---

## 19. Current Project Stage

The current project stage is:

> Sprint 1: Foundation Build

The current focus is:

1. Governance foundation.
2. Project identity foundation.
3. AI collaboration foundation.
4. Knowledge base foundation.
5. Preparation for structured product and architecture design.

Engineering implementation should not outrun foundation clarity.
